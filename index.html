What the process demands
On setups
Setups improve the probability of winning. They do not guarantee it. No setup works every time. My job is to execute the process correctly — the outcome is the market's business, not mine.
On size — the only rule
130 in the box. First. Always. I say it before I open the order window. I do not look at the premium and calculate what 5000 qty would make. That calculation is the enemy. It has cost me more than any bad setup ever has.
On small profits
30 points is not a small profit. It is the plan working. The feeling that it is not enough is the lie that has kept me in the loop for 2 years. I am happy and satisfied with every profit that comes from following the process.
On losing trades
A failed setup is not a failure of character. The response to a failed setup — that is character. I accept the small loss. I do not average down. I do not revenge trade. I wait for the next setup.
The loop I am breaking — today
For 2 years the scalper voice has won in the moment. 5000 qty × 10 pts = ₹50,000. That thought has cost me more than any market move. Today the voice fires and I say: 130 in the box. Points on the board. Week target in view. Then I execute or walk away.
Before the trading terminal — 5 commitments
✓
I have a setup — not a feeling
The setup exists on the chart before I open the order window. If I cannot describe it in one sentence, it is not a setup.
✓
SL is defined in points — before entry
Not "I'll see where it goes." Not a mental stop. A real number. Written or said out loud before the order is placed.
✓
Lot size is from the calculator — not gut
I open the calculator. I enter the SL. I use the recommended lots. 130 goes in the order box first. Any change upward requires a reason, not a feeling.
✓
Intraday or overnight — decided now, not later
If overnight: the hedge is planned before entry. Not "I'll hedge if it goes against me." That plan never survives contact with a moving position.
✓
Daily stop is ₹5,000 — I will honour it today
If ₹5,000 is gone, the terminal closes. No "one more trade to recover." No exceptions. The market will be there tomorrow. My capital must be too.
